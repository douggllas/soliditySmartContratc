Who can access a Smart Contract in a DVP?

It is important to remember that the Smart Contract in our case is the structure that will give access to the interface that will display a prescription. Thus, the prescription has two types of display credentials:

  • Visitor
  • Owner

Visitor

This user has the autonomy to only view what the contract owner allows, since the viewing of some information in the contract is configured by default, others can be restricted according to the owner's wishes.

Owner

As soon as the health professional (doctor or respective) generates the contract (prescription) he identifies in the system database who is the owner of that prescription. This, in turn, immediately becomes the owner of the contract and it is he who moderates this Smart Contract from now on. onwards. As said before, some information displayed on this document can be hidden the next time next time the contract is displayed by another health professional, such as pathologies, origin of the prescription, address address, or the patient's name.

Storage

Once created by a physician, a copy of the document in its original form is stored in a secure database (Smart Contract B). Another version of the document (Smart Contract A) is released for the edits and queries we spoke of earlier by the owner of the document.

Smart Contract B

This version of the document cannot be edited or viewed by the owner, it is available only to the owner database owner (Ministry of Health or Government agency). That is, even if it is available for consultation, the document can only be visualized if the owner has interest in doing so.

Edição do Smart Contract

Once the Smart Contract is created, it must be fed by the healthcare professional who created it, before identifying the owner. Since once the document has been transferred of ownership, it cannot be fed back. Only one change can be made to the document: personal data of the owner may or may not be hidden if ele tenha want to.

Viewing

The document can be viewed by three agents: owner, physician who generated the document, or other health professional authorized and accredited by the government.
